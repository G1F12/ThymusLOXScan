# Current Evidence Technical Appendix

## GSE240016 Primary Result Summary

The primary mouse result is a candidate-level GSE240016 annotated mTEC1 `Loxl2` transcript signal with lower detection in aged samples than young samples.

## mTEC1 Loxl2 Caveats

- n=2 young and n=2 aged biological samples.
- Sparse aged mTEC1 detection.
- Aged samples have lower depth/detected genes.
- Batch/sample confounding is not ruled out.
- No protein, spatial, function, activity, or causal experiment has been performed.

## Dropout/Depth Audit Summary

Dropout/depth artifact risk is partial/unresolved. Directional checks remain young-higher/aged-lower, but sparse detection and lower aged depth prevent stronger interpretation.

## Batch/Sample Confounding Status

Batch/sample confounding is not ruled out. Current wording should keep this as an unresolved limitation.

## Matched-Gene/Internal Falsification Summary

Available internal checks support cautious candidate prioritization but do not remove dropout, depth, or batch concerns.

## E-MTAB-8560 Summary

E-MTAB-8560 provides independent mTEC-focused transcript-level context. The clearest aged-lower `Loxl2` pattern appears in mTEClo and cTEC, with weaker support in mTEChi. Overall interpretation is mixed/inconclusive and not exact GSE240016 mTEC1 validation.

## Why Wet-Lab Is Needed

The current computational evidence is transcript-level and dataset-limited. Wet-lab LOXL2 IHC/IF, RNA-ISH, or sorted TEC/mTEC qPCR/ddPCR is needed to test whether the candidate signal is detectable and age-associated in mouse thymic epithelial/medullary regions.
