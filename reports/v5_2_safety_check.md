## Result

v5.2 is safe. No risky phrase is used to strengthen the mTEC1 `Loxl2` or LOXL2 protein/spatial claims.

## Flagged terms and disposition

- `mechanism`: used only in the Introduction as "not to prove a mechanism" and in a reference title. This is negated/background use, not a claim.
- `definitive`: used only in "rather than definitive evidence/inference" language. This is cautionary.
- `human conservation`: used only in statements that no human conservation claim is supported or made. This is cautionary.
- `robust`: appears as part of existing path-like labels such as `reports/mtec1_loxl2_robustness.md` and "Per-sample robustness plots". These are file/report descriptors, not claim language.
- `rejuvenation`: appears in the Limitations sentence stating that transcript abundance does not establish thymic rejuvenation. This is negated limitation language.
- `causal` / `causality`: used only to state that causality is not shown or resolved. This is cautionary.

## Explicitly avoided claims

The manuscript does not state that dropout is ruled out, that mTEC1 `Loxl2` is validated, that LOXL2 protein is reduced in aged mTEC1, that mouse mTEC1 protein localization is confirmed, that the result proves biology, that a mechanism is established, that ECM crosslinking/thymic function/rejuvenation/therapeutic relevance is established, that human conservation is supported, or that external datasets exactly replicate mTEC1.

## Final safety judgment

The v5.2 wording is not stronger than v5.1. It adds completed audit context while preserving the core classification that mTEC1 `Loxl2` is a transcript-level candidate with Partial / unresolved dropout/depth artifact risk and weak indirect protein/spatial support only.
